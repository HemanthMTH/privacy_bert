This interface is developed to visualize the results obtained by our CMSC 641 project ("Assessing Privacy Policy Evolution with BERT").

What we're doing here is pretty cool. We're using a powerful tool, called BERT, to compare the privacy policies of lots of different websites. Specifically, we're looking at how these policies have changed between 2016 and 2020. With BERT, we can tell how similar two pieces of text are - so we can see how much a policy has changed over time. By doing this, we can help people understand what's happening with online privacy, and which websites are making big changes.

Similarity Scores

The following chart you're looking at is called a tree map. It's showing how similar the privacy policies of 45 smart device manufacturers' websites are between 2016 and 2020. Each block or square on the chart is a different website. The color gradient of the block tells you how similar the two privacy policies of corresponding website are. The blocks are arranged from top to bottom based on how similar the policies are, with the most similar at the top. This helps you see which websites made big changes to their privacy policies over those four years, and which ones didn't change much at all.

Take a look below bar graph. It's a simple, straightforward way to understand our project results. On the X-axis at the bottom, you'll see different website labels - each one represents a different website. On the Y-axis, you'll see a number scale, these are the similarity scores. Each bar in the graph corresponds to a website. The height of the bar shows how similar the smart device manufacturer's website's privacy policy in 2020 is to its 2016 version. The taller the bar, the more similar the policies, meaning less change has happened over these four years. By looking at this graph, you can easily compare the changes in privacy policies across different websites.
